Grilled Teriyaki Salmon

Description

After years of hosting a dinner parties and cookouts, friends and family have come to expect teriyaki grilled salmon on our menu. Contrary to popular notion, salmon is actually one of the most effortless, no-sweat choices for everything from everyday family dinners to impressing dinner guests. Grilled salmon is not only super easy, it’s also healthy and flavorful. Everyone, from Littles and Bigs alike, will polish off this salmon like nobody’s business.

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets, 6-8 oz each, about 1-inch thick
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
Teriyaki Sauce
  • 1/2 cup Teriyaki Sauce (such as Kikkoman)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 white sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar, lightly packed
  • Juice of 2 fresh orange wedges
  • 1 1/2 TB cornstarch, fully dissolved in 1-2 TB water
  • Dash of ground ginger
  • Toasted sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Teriyaki Sauce: In a heavy medium pan, combine all sauce ingredients. Whisk to combine well. Bring to a boil. Lower to simmer and whisk until sauce is thickened, about 1 minute. Keep warm.
  2. Prep Salmon: thoroughly dry salmon fillets with paper towels. Sprinkle a light layer of kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides (or just top side, if bottom has skin on.)
  3. Cook: lightly grease and heat outdoor grill (or indoor stovetop grill pan) until very hot (smoky.) Lay salmon fillets on grill and cook on medium-high heat until just-cooked. Do not over-cook. Generally, about 2-3 min per side, depending on thickness. Let cooked fish rest at room temp for a few minutes before serving.
  4. Serve: drizzle generously with teriyaki sauce and garnish as desired. Serve immediately. Delicious over a bed of rice or over a plate of green salad.
